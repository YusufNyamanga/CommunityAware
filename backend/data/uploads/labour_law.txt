LABOUR LAW FOR THE PRIVATE SECTOR

Chapter I: Definitions and General Provisions

Article 1: In the application of the provisions of this Law, the following terms and expressions shall have the meanings assigned against each of them, unless the context requires otherwise:
(a) Worker: Any person, male or female, employed for a wage of any kind in the service of an employer and under his management or supervision.
(b) Employer: Any person or legal entity employing one or more workers for a wage of any kind.
(c) Establishment: Any economic, technical, industrial or commercial unit where workers are employed.

Article 2: The provisions of this Law shall apply to all employers and workers, except for:
(a) Civil servants and employees of public corporate entities
(b) Domestic servants and persons regarded as such
(c) Persons employed in temporary and seasonal agricultural work
(d) Persons employed on vessels with a tonnage of less than 500 tons

Chapter II: Employment of Workers

Article 3: Every employer shall send to the Ministry:
(a) A statement of the name of his establishment, its location, type of business, address, and number of workers employed.
(b) A statement showing every worker's name, job, nationality, wage, and date of employment.

Article 4: Every citizen of working age who is capable of and willing to work may apply for registration with the Ministry, specifying his age, qualifications, previous experience, and the type of work he desires.

Chapter III: Apprenticeship and Vocational Training

Article 5: An apprenticeship contract is a contract whereby a person commits himself to work for an employer for a specified period during which the employer undertakes to train him in a specific craft or occupation.

Chapter IV: Contract of Employment

Article 23: A contract of employment is an agreement between an employer and a worker whereby the latter undertakes to work in the service of the employer and under his management and supervision for a wage.

Article 24: The contract of employment shall be in writing and shall be drawn up in Arabic in three copies, one copy for each party and the third to be deposited with the Ministry. If the contract is written in a foreign language besides Arabic, the Arabic text shall prevail.

Chapter V: Wages

Article 38: Wages shall be fixed by individual or collective labor contracts. If the wage is not fixed by contract, the worker shall be entitled to a wage equivalent to that paid for similar work in the same establishment or occupation.

Article 39: Wages shall be paid in the legal tender of the country at least once a month to workers employed on a monthly basis and at least once every two weeks to other workers.

Chapter VI: Working Hours and Leave

Article 51: The maximum working hours shall be eight hours per day or forty-eight hours per week. During the month of Ramadan, working hours for Muslim workers shall be reduced to six hours per day and thirty-six hours per week.

Article 58: Every worker shall be entitled to a weekly rest day with full pay. Friday shall be the weekly rest day for all workers except those on a different system approved by the Ministry.

Article 116: A worker shall be entitled to annual leave with full pay of not less than thirty days for one year of service. If the period of service is less than one year, the worker shall be entitled to leave in proportion to the period of his service.

Article 117: The employer shall grant sick leave with full pay for the first fifteen days, with 3/4 pay for the next fifteen days, with 1/2 pay for the next fifteen days, and without pay for any subsequent periods.

Chapter VII: Employment of Women

Article 59: All provisions regulating the employment of workers shall apply to women workers without discrimination in similar work situations.

Article 63: A female worker shall be entitled to maternity leave of sixty days with full pay, including the period before and after delivery, provided she produces a medical certificate issued by a licensed medical practitioner.

Chapter VIII: Safety, Occupational Health and Social Care

Article 90: Every employer shall take the necessary precautions to protect workers during work from injury and occupational diseases.

Article 91: Every establishment employing fifty workers or more shall provide appropriate medical facilities managed by a qualified nurse. If the number of workers exceeds one hundred, the establishment shall employ a doctor in addition to the nurse.

Final Provisions

Article 185: Any condition which contravenes the provisions of this Law shall be null and void even if it precedes the date of its implementation unless it is more beneficial to the worker.

Article 186: This Law shall be published in the Official Gazette and shall come into effect from the date of its publication.
